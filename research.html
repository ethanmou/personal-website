Improving Mathematical Reasoning in Large Language Models

Shanghai AI Laboratory | Research Intern | Research internship

At Shanghai AI Laboratory, I worked on improving the mathematical reasoning ability of large language models. I conducted supervised fine-tuning on DeepSeek-R1-Distill-Qwen-7B using explicit Chain-of-Thought supervision to improve multi-step reasoning and alignment between intermediate reasoning and final answers. I built and preprocessed large-scale datasets for model pretraining and explored the use of Lean4-based formal verification for improving LLM mathematical reasoning.

Probing Video World Models for Physical Property Discovery

Priya Panda's Lab | Researcher | Ongoing research

In Priya Panda's Lab, I investigate whether video world models can be used to infer physical properties of objects from images and predicted motion. My work focuses on probing the Stanford NeuroAI Lab's PSI world model through counterfactual interventions, such as applying sparse flow constraints or virtual perturbations to an object and analyzing the model's predicted pixel-level changes. I also study visual vibrometry methods, including complex steerable pyramids and phase-based motion extraction, to connect predicted motion signals with material properties such as stiffness, elasticity, compressibility, and rigidity. Through this project, I aim to understand whether world models can support physical property discovery from visual data.
