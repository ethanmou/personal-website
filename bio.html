Bio

Academic Biography

I am an undergraduate student at the University of Southern California, where I am pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Applied and Computational Mathematics. My academic interests include artificial intelligence, machine learning, computational mathematics, applied statistics, large language models, mathematical reasoning, formal verification, data-driven modeling, remote sensing, geospatial analysis, and visual world models.

My research experience spans several areas of computational and data-driven inquiry. I have worked on improving mathematical reasoning in large language models through supervised fine-tuning, Chain-of-Thought supervision, dataset preparation, and the study of Lean4-based formal verification. I have also processed and analyzed environmental datasets, including satellite imagery, geospatial layers, and time-series data, with a focus on remote sensing and land cover change analysis.

I am also interested in visual world models and their potential role in physical property discovery from visual data. My work in this area examines how predicted motion, counterfactual interventions, and phase-based motion extraction may help connect visual signals with material properties. More broadly, I am interested in computational approaches to scientific and interdisciplinary problems, especially where mathematical modeling and machine learning can support careful analysis of complex data.
