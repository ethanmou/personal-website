Bio

Academic Biography

Xingyun Mou is an undergraduate student at the University of Southern California, where he is pursuing a Bachelor of Science in Applied and Computational Mathematics. His academic interests include artificial intelligence, machine learning, computational mathematics, applied statistics, large language models, mathematical reasoning, formal verification, data-driven modeling, remote sensing, geospatial analysis, and visual world models.

His research experience spans several areas of computational and data-driven inquiry. He has worked on improving mathematical reasoning in large language models through supervised fine-tuning, Chain-of-Thought supervision, dataset preparation, and the study of Lean4-based formal verification. He has also processed and analyzed environmental datasets, including satellite imagery, geospatial layers, and time-series data, with a focus on remote sensing and land cover change analysis.

Xingyun is also interested in visual world models and their potential role in physical property discovery from visual data. His work in this area examines how predicted motion, counterfactual interventions, and phase-based motion extraction may help connect visual signals with material properties. More broadly, he is interested in computational approaches to scientific and interdisciplinary problems, especially where mathematical modeling and machine learning can support careful analysis of complex data.
